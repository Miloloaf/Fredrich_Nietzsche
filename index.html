Friedrich Nietzsche

the German Philosopher


Timeline of Nietzsche's life:

  • 1844 - Birth of Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche on October 15 in Rocken.
  • 1849 - Father's death on July 30.
  • 1850 - Brother's death on January 9. Family relocate to Naumburg an der Saale.
  • 1856 - "On the Origin of Evil", Nietzsche's first philosophical essay, was written.
  • 1858 - Accepted into Pforta School in October.
  • 1862 - Founds the literary club "Germania"
  • 1864 - Enters the University of Bonn as a theology and philology student.
  • 1865 - Persues philology at the University of Leipzig. Discovers Schopenhauer's "The World as Will and Representation".
  • 1867 - Enters mandatory military service to an equestrial field artillery regiment. Nietzche suffers a serious chest injury and was on sick leave.
  • 1868 - Befriends Richard Wagner, the German composer.
  • 1869 - Professor at the University of Basel.
  • 1870 - Military service as a medic. Contracts dysentry and diphtheria.
  • 1872 - Published "The Birth of Tragedy" which had a critical reception.
  • 1876 - Relationship with Wagner terminates.
  • 1879 - Neccessary resignation from University of Basel due to deterioration health. Nietzsche starts a nomadic lifestyle.

  • 1889 - Nietzche has a mental breakdown on January 3 and never recovers.
  • 1900 - August 25, Nietzche's death at 55.
